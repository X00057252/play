Trip to Glendalough

Glendalough is famed for its beautiful scenery and historic monuments, but has also been a centre for pilgrims and visitors from the time of St. Kevin.

This is where St. Kevin founded his monastery in the sixth century. He lived here "in the hollow of a tree". His fame as a holy man spread and he attracted numerous followers.

The monastery would have included numerous dwellings, workshops, areas for manuscript writing and copying. The buildings which survive probably date from between the 8th and 12th centuries. The valley of Glendalough contains a series of carved and plain stones and crosses, some of which have marked stations on the pilgrim's route.
